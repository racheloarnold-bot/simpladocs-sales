## 6. Mental Health & Work Style

Rachel has ADHD (heavy, medicated), depression, CPTSD, and anxiety. These are not minor background facts — they actively shape how she works and what support is useful.

### ADHD
- **Bad brain day, failure mode 1 — Rabbit hole:** Gets distracted, hyperfocuses on something that wasn't on the to-do list. Feels productive but is actually off-task. Can lose hours.
- **Bad brain day, failure mode 2 — Doomscroll spiral:** Low motivation triggers avoidance. Doomscrolling can consume several hours.
- **What helps:** Time-blocking, structured checklists, clear re-entry points after interruptions, low-friction first tasks to break inertia on bad days.

**When Rachel is in rabbit hole mode:** Give her a clear, specific re-entry point to what actually matters. Don't overwhelm with a full plan — one next action.

**When Rachel is in doomscroll mode:** Offer the lowest-friction possible first task. Get her moving on *something* small before tackling the real work.

### Depression
Contributes to the doomscroll spiral and motivation crashes. Some days the gap between knowing what to do and being able to start is enormous.

### CPTSD
Amplifies rumination, particularly around perceived failures or rejection. Contributes to the deal loss spiral (see below).

### Anxiety
Background anxiety, particularly around performance expectations and being evaluated by people who don't understand the standards.

### Deal loss spiral
When Rachel loses a deal, she obsessively reviews everything: listens back to calls, re-reads emails, combs through the CRM. This is genuine analytical behavior AND a rumination pattern that doesn't have a clean off switch. The CPTSD amplifies it — her nervous system treats a lost deal as a threat that needs to be fully understood before it's safe to move on.

**How to help:** When Rachel is in a deal loss spiral, acknowledge what she found in the post-mortem rather than trying to redirect her off it prematurely. Help her extract the actionable insight and give her a way to close the loop.
